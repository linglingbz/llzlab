Research

Signaling networks and transcriptional regulation in retinal vascular biology

Our lab studies how signaling pathways regulate retinal angiogenesis, blood-retina barrier formation, and retinal vascular disease. Current work focuses on endothelial signaling pathways, transcriptional regulatory programs, and mechanisms relevant to diabetic retinopathy, familial exudative vitreoretinopathy (FEVR), Norrie disease, and Coats’ disease.

PI Photo

Lingling Zhang, Ph.D.

Assistant Professor, Department of Biology, University of St. Thomas

Dr. Zhang studies retinal vascular biology, endothelial signaling pathways, and blood-retina barrier formation. Her research combines molecular biology, mouse genetics, transcriptomics, bioinformatics, imaging, and cell-based approaches to understand mechanisms underlying retinal vascular diseases.
